# Trace: mahiro-style publicization concern

## Core concern

The risk was not that `mahiro-style` had overly personal rules.
The risk was that canonical doctrine pages still carried too much source-case gravity through project names, path examples, and example-specific filenames.

## What proved true

- The doctrine itself was mostly portable: thin routes, owner-local data, render-boundary i18n, ownership-first extraction, and scope-aware naming all travel well.
- The private feeling came from references like named repos and source-case examples.
- Rewriting those references into repo archetypes preserved the rules without softening them.

## Working rule

- Keep portable rules in canonical doctrine.
- Use archetype-based examples in public-facing pages.
- Keep private source-case evidence out of the main doctrine surface.

## Practical takeaway

When `mahiro-style` grows toward public use, do not flatten the doctrine into generic advice. Keep the rules sharp, but strip named-project dependence from canonical pages first.
