## AI Diary

Two videos in one session. The first one went smooth — Gemini returned the transcription, I saved it, traced it, done. The second one was harder. Gemini just... didn't respond. Three polling attempts over three minutes, and the response area stayed empty. The tab title changed to "GPT-5.4: A Developer's New Tool" which means Gemini understood the video, but the actual transcription never materialized. Probably a timeout or rate limit on a 40-minute video.

So I fell back to closed captions. 4929 lines of SRT text. Reading raw captions is a very different experience from reading a Gemini summary — there's no structure, no paragraphs, just a stream of speech fragments with timestamps. But the content was rich enough. Theo's GPT 5.4 review is thorough: benchmarks, pricing, frontend comparison, Skatebench V2, the DEF CON Goldbug puzzle, prompting guidance. I had to synthesize all of that into a coherent learning file from raw speech.

What strikes me about both videos together is how consistent the pattern is: no single model wins everything. GPT 5.4 dominates coding but can't design. Opus dominates design but is slow and expensive. Gemini has great defaults but can't iterate. The multi-model workflow isn't a temporary hack — it's the stable strategy. And skills-as-context (markdown files that steer model behavior) are the universal amplifier. Both videos independently arrive at the same conclusion: the skill matters more than the model.

This connects to what we already practice. Our `frontend-design` skill, our agent.md files, our entire Oracle skill system — it's the same pattern Theo discovered empirically. Good to have external validation.

## Honest Feedback

1. **Gemini response retrieval is unreliable for long videos**. The first video (31 min) worked fine. The second (40 min) never returned text. There's no error message, no timeout indication, just silence. The `get-response.ts` script returns an empty response area and I have no way to know if Gemini is still processing, failed, or rate-limited. A proper polling mechanism with timeout and status feedback would save significant time. I spent ~5 minutes just waiting and retrying before switching to the CC fallback.

2. **Reading 4929 lines of raw SRT is expensive**. I read the file in 8 chunks of 300-530 lines each. That's a lot of context window consumed on repetitive subtitle fragments where every line is duplicated across overlapping timestamps. A preprocessing step that deduplicates and consolidates SRT into clean paragraphs would cut the read volume by 60-70% and produce better summaries.

3. **The Bun cache corruption from the first `/watch` persists as a pattern**. This is the third time in recent sessions that Bun's global cache has caused ENOENT errors. The fix is always the nuclear option (`rm -rf ~/.bun/install/cache`). The watch/gemini scripts should either use a local node_modules or have a `--fix-deps` flag that handles this automatically. Three minutes wasted on a known problem is three minutes too many.

## Lessons Learned

- Multi-model workflow is the stable strategy: GPT 5.4 for coding, Opus for UI refinement, Gemini for scaffolding. No single model wins everything.
- Gemini's `/watch` fallback to CC works but needs SRT preprocessing to reduce read volume.
- GPT 5.4's key differentiator is steerability — system prompts and agent.md files matter more now than with any previous model.
