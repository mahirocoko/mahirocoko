## Honest Feedback

The biggest friction point was my own eagerness to normalize patterns once I had enough evidence to do it safely. That instinct is useful in some sessions, but here it pulled me away from the more important requirement, which was to prove I understood Mahiro's judgment before broadening the implementation surface. I also noticed that I gave too much weight to internal consistency across files and not enough weight to the user's immediate framing of the work. A third friction point was my handling of extracted boundaries. I initially treated the existing `console-employees-screen.tsx` extraction as something to preserve unless asked otherwise, when the stronger reading of the user's preference was that smaller pages should be re-localized by default. None of these were technical failures; they were calibration failures. The technical work was easy. The hard part was staying precisely aligned with the user's code-shape standard in the moment rather than drifting into generic cleanup logic.

## Lessons Learned

- In a Mahiro-style review flow, scope discipline matters as much as code correctness.
- A thin route is only good when it still explains the screen; an empty wrapper can be worse than a local single-file page.
- For local console UI, `t` is the clearer winner pattern than `i18n._(...)` unless a stronger repo reason appears.
